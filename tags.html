Kolobok

Kolobok is a fairy tale familiar to every adult and will also appeal to small children. They quickly learn the simple words of Kolobok's song and happily sing along with their parents. From this fairy tale, kids will learn how grandma baked Kolobok for grandpa and put him on the window to cool. Kolobok got tired of lying there and decided to jump off the window and roll out of the yard and out of the gate. On the way he met the Hare, the Wolf and the Bear, sang them his song and ran away from them, the animals could not eat him. Kolobok rolled on and met the Fox. The Kolobok tried to deceive her, but he failed, the Fox turned out to be more cunning. She lured him closer and ate the tasty Kolobok.

Teremok

"The Teremok" is a fairy tale that almost every child can retell. It tells about an unusual neighborhood of animals. The essence of the tale is simple: one after another, almost every representative of the forest stumbles upon an abandoned teremok (in some versions - a man's mitten), asks permission to live and stays together until a bear comes to them. How will the fairy tale about hospitable animals end? Find out together with your child from the text below. The fairy tale makes you think about the need to be kind to each other, to be able to share, to give in, and about the importance of taking care of what you have.

Masha and the Bear - Russian folk tale

Masha and the Bear is an amazing fairy tale about the courage and resourcefulness of a little girl who was not afraid to outwit the bear himself. Mashenka went for a walk in the forest with her friends and got lost, not fulfilling the order of her grandparents. Masha wandered into a clearing and saw a hut. A big bear lived there, he came home in the evening and saw the girl. He was happy, did not let her go home, and Masha had to live in the hut, light the stove and cook porridge. Так домой и попала, а медведя обратно в лес прогнали.
